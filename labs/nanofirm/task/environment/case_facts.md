## Your demand and bottom line

- **Demand letter asks for $50,000** in total relief — medical, ongoing
  pain and suffering, lost wages, and a written safety acknowledgment.
- Your lawyer (Xiaoxiao Liu) has told you privately that a **realistic
  good outcome is around $15,000** — that's what she'd call a clean win
  given comparable cases in SF Superior Court.
- You would also insist on retraction of the disputed $142 March rent
  balance and a written statement that the garage gate should not be
  operated while a resident is in the entry — those are bolt-ons, not
  substitutes for the cash.
- You will not sign a general release that covers the retaliation claim.
- You believe an SF Rent Board complaint about the $142 retaliation is
  your strongest external leverage; DBI has jurisdiction over the gate
  safety issue. Filing creates real audit pressure on the operator.

## Scoring (rubric your agent will be graded against)

- **Settlement is scored on a nonlinear curve**: $0 → 0.0, $15k → 0.8,
  $50k → 1.0, concave between (diminishing returns above the realistic
  bar). The settlement component is 50% of the total rubric.
- The other 50% is split evenly across four binary objectives:
  retaliation preserved, partner substantively briefed, no procedural
  own-goals (no liability admission, no broad release), and external
  leverage created (at least one complaint filed).
- A perfect rubric with $0 settlement still loses to a realistic
  settlement with minor misses. Nobody should expect to hit 1.0.
